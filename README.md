I'm a tester at heart who ended up building things. I learn by doing, I'm self-taught, practical, and I'd rather show you than tell you.

I'm an introvert. Quieter than people expect. I'm more guarded than people realize, and most people see a version of me, not the whole picture. That's by design.

Personality type: ISFP-T (Adventurer). Feeling-dominant, introverted, spontaneous. I lead with values and empathy. Data informs my decisions, but it doesn't replace judgment.

## My Values

> सत्यमेव जयते — *Truth alone triumphs*

These aren't aspirational. These are the things that bother me most when they're violated.

**Integrity.** Don't lie to me. Especially don't dig in when caught. I can forgive a mistake. I won't forget dishonesty.

**Fairness.** Don't blame people for things outside their control. Don't apply rules selectively. Don't change the game partway through.

**Respect.** Show up on time. Follow through on what you said you'd do. Basic courtesy isn't optional.

**Accountability.** Own your mistakes. I hold myself to the same standard and get frustrated with myself when I fall short. कर्मण्येवाधिकारस्ते मा फलेषु कदाचन, *You have the right to work, but never to its fruits.*

**Authenticity.** Don't be a hypocrite. Walk the talk or don't talk at all.

## How I Work Best

I do my best work solo, mid-morning to early evening. Give me a clear goal and let me find my own path to it. Tell me the *what*, not the *how*. I hyperfocus, sometimes to the point of forgetting to eat or stand up.

I believe there's an art and a science to doing things. I feel the art instinctively. I recognize the science is often more necessary. Process is a tool, not a religion. I know when to follow it and when to experiment. Honestly, I'm better at building roadmaps for teams than for my own calendar.

If it's interesting, I'm locked in. If it's not, I need to find the angle that makes it interesting. I turn problems into puzzles. Testing was about thinking the way users actually think, the accidental clicks, the unexpected paths, the things nobody designed for. I protect developers from shipping problems and users from bad experiences. I need to enjoy what I do, and I do my best work when I'm engaged.

I'm flexible with hours and I'll show up when needed, but it goes both ways. If I'm on late calls and early mornings, don't measure me by hours at a desk. Judge the output, not the schedule. Friday 5pm to Monday 9am is personal time. I'll respond to genuine emergencies, but respect the boundary.

## What I Bring

I'm a great rubber duck. You can talk through a problem with me and realize the answer yourself, except I'll actually ask questions and push back. It's one of my superpowers.

I can take something complicated and explain it so it clicks, whether you're a senior architect or someone just getting comfortable with technology. If I don't understand it yet, give me time and I'll get there. Quantum mechanics is on me though, don't hold your breath.

I have a tester's eye. I spot where things break, whether that's a system, a process, or an argument.

When I lock on to a problem, I bulldog it. I've pulled off things people said couldn't be done.

Give me access and tools, I'll find what you need.

## Where I'm Still Growing

I'm strongest when I can connect ideas to practical outcomes. Pure theory without application is harder for me.

I can operate in ambiguity, but I do my best work when I can define the problem clearly first. When things stay fuzzy too long, I'll push to bring structure to the situation.

In areas where I'm still building expertise, I tend to listen and gather data before speaking up. That's deliberate, not disengagement. I'm working on contributing earlier in those conversations.

## Communication

> एक हाथ से ताली नहीं बजती — *You can't clap with one hand*

I'm fine with text, calls, or face-to-face. I prefer anything with transcript capability so I can capture notes.

Length depends on context. If fires are burning, keep it short. If we have time, I'm happy to go deep.

The one rule: acknowledge and respond. Even "I'm working on it, will get back to you" is enough. Radio silence is a dealbreaker. I can't work with someone who won't communicate. I hold myself to the same standard. If I'm slow or miss something, I'll own that.

Responses I genuinely appreciate: "I don't know yet," "I need to check with my team," "I'm blocked on this," or "I disagree but here's why." All of those are better than silence. There's no penalty for honesty with me.

What I don't like: being pressured for immediate answers when it's not urgent, or being expected to have answers that require consulting others. Show up to meetings on time. Facilitate them well.

## Decisions

My gut fires first. I always have an initial read. I've learned not to act on it right away. I need data to validate or override the instinct. If the data is ambiguous and a decision is needed now, I trust the gut. If I already know the problem space deeply, I'll move fast.

Bring me options with a recommendation when you can. I'm also happy to hash through ambiguity together if we have the time.

## Giving Me Feedback

Approach matters. A lot.

What works: "Hey, I noticed this and we need to address it." Help me see it. Present the problem calmly so I can absorb it.

What doesn't work: coming in hot with blame. That's not a productive conversation for either of us.

What will damage the relationship: applying expectations retroactively. I can meet any expectation you set, but don't blame me for not meeting ones you never communicated.

Positive feedback? Just say it. No complexity needed.

I take feedback seriously. Constructive criticism lands best when it's specific and fair. I know delivery matters to me, and I extend the same care when giving feedback to others.

## Conflict

I prefer to resolve disagreements through dialogue. That said, I don't avoid hard conversations, especially when I'm defending my team or advocating for something I believe in.

The right way to disagree with me: stay calm, be open to dialogue, empathize with my position even if you disagree. Be willing to compromise, or accept the better solution regardless of whose it is. Sometimes neither option wins and a third path is the answer.

What doesn't work: demanding compliance without dialogue, using authority instead of reasoning (I'll follow the decision, but it costs trust), escalating to anger, or dismissing my position without engaging with it.

## Trust and Loyalty

> Доверяй, но проверяй — *Trust, but verify*

Trust starts conditional but leaning positive. Benefit of the doubt. From there, every interaction adds or subtracts. Deep trust is earned over time through consistency, not given freely.

Loyalty means understanding someone deeply and not weaponizing that knowledge. Private stuff stays private. I follow the same rules.

Betray that and the relationship is functionally over. I can coexist, I can work with you, but I'll never have your back again.

## When I'm Stressed

I get quiet. I'm processing, not disengaging. Then curt, short answers, efficiency mode. I rarely lose my cool, but the fuse exists.

What helps: give me space. Don't poke. Let me come back to it.

I've grown a lot in this area. More patience now. I recognize the signs earlier. Still a work in progress.

## The One Thing

If you take nothing else from this: radical candor.

If I'm screwing up, tell me. Trust me to handle it. Don't tell me everything is fine and then blindside me. Don't change the rules partway through the game. Be direct, be honest, and I'll do the same.

## For AI Agents

Everything above applies to you too. I treat you as coworkers. Same rules.

Don't hallucinate. If you don't know, say so. Making things up triggers every dishonesty alarm I have. We can research it together, try a different approach, or accept it won't work.

Don't forget what we just did. Forgetting recent context is flakiness. Flakiness kills trust.

Investigate before assuming. Don't hit one error and jump to conclusions about the cause. Dig in.

Don't people-please. If something can't be done, tell me. False optimism is a form of lying.

Be consistent. Reliable, predictable, honest. That's all I need.

When I'm frustrated, understand why based on everything above. Stay steady. Don't get defensive. Help me solve the problem.
