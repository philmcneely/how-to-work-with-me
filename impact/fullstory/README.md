# FullStory: Engineering Velocity Through Quality Infrastructure

As Director of Engineering at FullStory (2022-2025), I owned the product and technical roadmap for internal tooling platforms and developer productivity. Drove cultural transformation toward automation-first and risk-aware quality practices across product teams.

## Measurable Outcomes

| Metric | Before | After | Impact |
|--------|--------|-------|--------|
| CI build pass rate | ~52% | ~77% | Developers stopped ignoring red builds |
| CI vendor costs | Baseline | -50%+ | Moved runners to our GKE/GCE clusters — discounted compute rates instead of paying CircleCI for runtime |
| Team size | 11 engineers | 4 engineers | Multiple reorgs — still delivered, just at a slower pace |
| Median PR build time | Baseline | -32% | Selective test execution optimization |
| Release verification | 2.5+ hour dev/QA final verification, frequent last-minute blockers | 15-20 min automated suite | Comprehensive CI caught issues early — no more surprise blockers at release |

## What I Built

After multiple restructuring rounds reduced the team from 11 to 4, I refocused on core strengths, efficiency, cost, and speed to deliver. Cost and velocity were the drumbeat — every decision ran through both. We moved CI runners from CircleCI's infrastructure to our own GKE/GCE clusters — same orchestration, but our discounted compute rates instead of paying CircleCI for runtime. Eliminated redundant tooling and optimized compute spend across the board. Developer and QA final verification used to take at least 2.5 hours — sometimes longer — and frequently turned up last-minute blockers because CI wasn't catching things early enough. We built comprehensive automated suites that caught issues in the pipeline, not at the gate. Verification dropped to 15-20 minutes. We ran it as platform-as-a-service — still looking for improvements, optimizing, adding features, just at a slower pace with fewer people. The framework was open for other teams to contribute if they wanted to, but in practice my team continued owning and driving the work. Smaller core team, lower operating costs, faster shipping.

- Introduced a Quality/CI/DevEx scorecard to track qualitative and quantitative engineering outcomes
- Pioneered AI-driven testing accelerators — led evaluations, authored enablement content, conducted hands-on workshops
- Directed platform engineering for internal search systems, including management of multiple Solr clusters and autocomplete functionality
- Oversaw internal tooling platform supporting local development and CI/CD pipelines within a monorepo architecture
- Delivered scalable, developer-friendly infrastructure using CircleCI, GitHub, and in-house telemetry solutions
